Successful launch of SAP Next-Gen in New York with HPI; ready for joint launch of SAP Leonardo Centers at SAPPHIRE
John Baxter 7 months ago Creator: John Baxter 209 Views 0 Likes

Under the leadership of Ann Rosenberg, the SAP Next-Gen team completed a very successful launch of SAP Next-Gen in New York in partnership with Hasso Plattner Institute which took place from April 26th to 30th on the 48th floor of SAP’s New York office at Hudson Yards.

 

From a Winners Dream event with Bill McDermott, to a 300 person event with academia, startups, tech community and purpose partners, and press, and concluding with a 3 day hackathon with SAP’s customer NASA, SAP Next-Gen is ready for the joint launch of the SAP Leonardo Center in New York and other global locations at SAPPHIRE NOW.

 

 

The opening was the result of a collaboration with Prof. Dr. Christoph Meinel and Dr. Matthias Uflacker of HPI, as well as Prof. Dr. Gesche Joost of Berlin University of the Arts and member of the SAP Supervisory Board, and the strong support of Sinead Kaiya, Vanessa Smith, Soledad Alvarado Ganzhorn, Bernd Welz, Peter Rasper, and many SAP teams including Leonardo, AppHaus Network, Ariba, Center for Digital Leadership, Digitalist, d-shop, GCA, GFM, Global Marketing, Hybris, ICN, Industries, P&I, SAP.io, SAP’s Apple team, Startup Focus, XM and many more.

Ann welcomed 300 attendees across morning and afternoon launch events, including academia, purpose driven partners, tech community partners, startups, venture firms, press, and SAP colleagues. They heard how SAP Next-Gen drives “Innovation with Purpose” linked to the 17 UN Global Goals. And they learned about new opportunities for customers and partners in New York and globally to join SAP Next-Gen through memberships in 25 industry clubs and 6 exponential technology clubs, where they can crowdsource insights from students, academic thought leaders and researchers, seed in disruptive innovation from startups, and connect with SAP experts and partners on the future of industries with exponential technologies.

We were thrilled with the passion of everyone who joined the event, and heard a consistent enthusiasm for joining together in an open community to work on “Innovation with Purpose” linked to the 17 UN Global Goals.

SAP thought leaders who inspired the audience from New York and beyond included Maggie Chan Jones, Sinead Kaiya, Pat Bakey, Deepak Krishnamurthy, Ram Jambunathan, Andreas Hauser and Falk Rieker. 

Partners who spoke included: 

  • Academia: Prof. Dr. Christoph Meinel and Dr. Matthias Uflacker of HPI; and Prof. Dr. Gesche Joost of Berlin University of the Arts and member of the SAP Supervisory Board. 
  • Tech community partners: Sapphire Ventures, RocketSpace, Tech:NYC, German Accelerator, German Center for Research and Innovation, NY Tech Alliance, Rise New York, and Startupbootcamp
  • Purpose driven partners: UN Women, Copenhagen Institute of Interaction Design, UNLEASH, and New York Academy of Sciences

Partners and SAP innovation teams who were represented in the space included: Leonardo, Apple and a showcase on iOS enterprise apps; SAP Hybris Labs, SAP XM, SAP Innovation Center Network, SAP.io, SAP Store, Digitalist Magazine, SAP AppHaus Network, SAP d-shop, SAP America, SAP Industries, and more.

Attendees had the opportunity to interact with installations and demos in the space including: 

  • SAP XM on the transformation of the digital media industry
  • SAP Hybris future of Customer Engagement and Commerce showcases including Pepper robot and Hybris Bullseye, Expose, Infinite Cart, Immersive Galaxy VR, and Party Booth
  • iOS Enterprise apps showcasing SAP’s partnership with Apple
  • Virtual Reality lounge
  • SAP Innovation Center Network Kaleidoscope featuring innovation projects such as SAP Clea for Resume Matching, Customer Retention Insights, Job Matching, Chatbot Application, and Machine Learning Games
  • SAP d-shop maker space including 3-D printers, Arduinos, and other maker technologies
  • SAP IoT Accelerator installations
  • SAP Digital Boardroom for 17 UN Global Goals
  • SAP Center for Digital Leadership demo of European Space Agency solutions
  • SAP Store
  • Preview of opportunities for customers to participate in design thinking workshops with the upcoming HPI d.school in New York

  • Check out the press coverage including SAP Next-Gen Banks on NYC Ingenuity for Innovation in IT, SAP Rises, Next-gen technologies displayed at SAP Innovation Center, and SAP is tapping the expertise of New York City area universities.

Introduction to the SAP's newest innovation space on the 48th floor at Hudson Yards in New York Highlights from the April 27th opening of SAP Next-Gen in New York in partnership with Hasso Plattner Institute

 

As part of the opening festivities, 140 students and faculty from Columbia, Rutgers, CUNY, Asia School of Business, BTECH, Queensborough Community College, and Delaware State University had the opportunity to meet Bill McDermott during a special "Winners Dream” event which took place April 26th on the 48th floor at Hudson Yards. After experiencing the “future of industries with exponential technologies” installations and demos on the 48th floor, the students and faculty had the privilege to meet Bill and hear his inspiring message on leadership, growth and career success.

“I have been checking out many industry innovation spaces in and around New York, and SAP’s new space at Hudson Yards is by far the most inspiring,” shared Moorissa Tjokro, a student at Columbia’s Data Science Institute.

We invite you to view the photo book and view two videos.

Bill's full Winners Dream message to students and faculty Highlights of Bill's Winners Dream message to students and faculty

 

Also as part of the SAP Next-Gen opening in New York, the team hosted the NASA Space Apps Challenge in New York, where from April 28th to 30th, students, developers and professionals came together on the 48th floor of SAP Hudson Yards for a 3 day hackathon to “Innovate with Purpose” and develop solutions to NASA challenges in space and on earth. View the photo book. 

SAP Next-Gen also hosted NASA Space Apps Challenge locations in Silicon Valley and in Bangalore.

 

To learn more and to be part of the SAP Next-Gen "Innovation with Purpose" movement...

  • Explore the new SAP Next-Gen website including our events page and calendar of upcoming SAP Next-Gen events
  • Join the SAP Next-Gen Global Challenges where startups, universities, industries, and SAP innovators can come together to develop disruptive solutions to the 17 UN Global Goals. During the SAP Next-Gen Global Challenges Summit in New York in fall 2017, thought leaders will share insights on enablers of change and innovation needed to accelerate progress on the Global Goals, and top teams will pitch their solutions across all 17 of the Global Goals. 
  • Join the many colleagues who are already SAP Next-Gen Innovation Ambassadors in New York and globally
  • Follow SAP Next-Gen on Twitter, Facebook, YouTube, and Instagram
  • Watch for the upcoming book “SAP Next-Gen: Innovation with Purpose” co-authored by Dr. Bernd Welz and Ann Rosenberg
  • Follow and be part of SAP Next-Gen meetups and upcoming events. The next events in New York are SAP Next-Gen After Party Celebrating NASA Space Apps May 2nd and SAP CodeJam May 9th. 

We look forward to the announcement of the SAP Leonardo Center in New York and other locations at SAPPHIRE NOW, and to strongly integrating SAP Next-Gen with all the SAP Leonardo Centers.

 

 Yours Ann  

Ann Rosenberg

Vice President & Global Head

SAP Next-Gen - Innovation with Purpose

Explore the new SAP Next-Gen website at http://www.sap.com/next-gen
